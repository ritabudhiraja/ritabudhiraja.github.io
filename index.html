Q.1 : Where did the ceremony takes place ?Can you name any public building in India that are made of sandstone?

Ans : The ceremonies took place in the amphitheatre of the union buildings of Pretoria. In India the parliament House, the Rashtrapati Bhavana, the Supreme Court of India in Delhi and Madras High court in Chennai are some examples of Indian public buildings that are made of sandstone.

Q.2 : Can you say how 10 May is an Autumn Day in South Africa?

Ans : 10 may is known as an Autumn Day in South Africa because on this day, there was the largest gathering of international leaders on South African soil for the installation of South Africa's first democratic, non racial government.

Q.3 : At the beginning of his speech, Mandela mentions "an extraordinary human disaster" what does he mean by this? What is the glorious --------human achievements" he speaks of at the end?

Ans : By "an extraordinary human disaster" Mandela means the inhuman practice of apartheid in South Africa. It was a racial discrimination suffered by the blacks at the hands of whites in South Africa. On 10 May there was an establishment of first democratic non- racial government, where the president was a black .So Nelson Mandela considered it as" glorious human achievement."

Q.4 : What does Mandela thank the international leaders for?

Ans : On 10 May 1994, there was an establishment of first democratic non- racial government where the president was black. Most of the countries dignitaries were present to attend the inauguration ceremony .Before this the South Africans were considered outlaws. So Nelson Mandela ,the president of first democratic non-racial government of South Africa felt honoured to be the host of the nations of the world. He considered that event as a common victory for justice peace and human dignity.

Q.5 : What ideals does he set out for the future of South Africa?

Ans : He ( Nelson Mandela) sets out the ideas of poverty alleviation, removal of suffering of people . He also dreams of a society where there would be no discrimination based on gender or racial origins.

Q.6 : What do the military generals do? How has their attitude changed and Why?

Ans : The military generals of the South African defence force and police saluted Mandela and pledge their loyalty. Their attitude towards black was changed.Instead of arresting ,they saluted him. If it was the apartheid Era, they would definitely arrest him . Their change attitude was because there was no apartheid system.There was democracy in South Africa. Now a black was their president .The struggle of blacks not only ensured the freedom of South Africa but also brought a change in the mindsets of many.

Q.7 : Why were two national anthems sung?

Ans : On the day of inauguration, two national anthems were sung. The interpretation of National anthem from old republic as well as new republicwas the sign of the pledge of those who were laying the foundation of new nation. As it was a pleasure to build a society devoid of any type of discrimination, symbolising the equality of blacks and whites and to show that it will be a truly multicultural society.

Q.8 : How does Mandela describe the systems of government in his country (1) in first decade (2)in the final decade of the 20th century?

Ans : (1) in the first decade of the 20 century the white skinned people of South Africa patched up their differences and erected a system of racial domination against the dark skinned people of their own land , thus creating the basis of one of the harshest and most inhumane societies the world had ever known.

(2) in the last decade of the 20th century, the previous system had been replaced by another more strong pattern in which there was a democratic system ,the one that recognised the rights and freedom of all people regardless of the colour of their skin.

Q.9 : What does courage mean to Mandela?

Ans : For Mandela courage does not mean the absence of fear but a victory over fear. According to him brave men need not only be fearless but should be able to conquer fear.

Q.10 : Which does he think is natural to love or to hate?

Ans : He believes loves comes more naturally to humans as opposed to hate. No one is born with hatred in his heart for another. People are educated to hate here if they can be educated for hate, they can be for love too and it comes more naturally.
